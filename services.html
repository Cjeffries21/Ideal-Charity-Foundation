Home based care for the Older, Frail and Disabled people

Overall, we feel that we can create the best experience for our community. We know that is a distinction that is very important for families looking into Older People Homebased care for their parents. Our services are for residents who need help with older people /disabled home based care, such as meals, pharmaceutical management, hygiene care, and everyday tasks that have become difficult. We find that our residents enjoy their time with our Homebased Caregivers because we provide a constant level of care and compassion along with the freedom for them to choose activities they are interested in. We feel that each resident whom we care for becomes part of our family. We also offer pharmaceutical management and monitoring to ensure our residents take their medicines on time daily. We also monitor health concerns, take older / disabled people for hospital check-ups, collect medication from our local Clinic for our residents.

Homework & Aftercare

We provide personal attention to the children at our Homework & Aftercare classes. Aftercare for children age 5 to 16 years, from 12 am to 16:30 pm, Monday to Friday. We have 70 active children at our Homework & Aftercare center and its growing by the day. Every effort is made to ensure our children access academic opportunities to excel as productive members of the Riverlea Community. We are enthusiastic about the establishment of a much needed Homework & Aftercare Center for the Riverlea community. We believe it will improve education gaps in our community. Ideal Charity Foundation aim to provide children with caring, happy environment where they can develop as individuals socially, intellectually, physically and emotionally. We also provide our children with a snack, drink, fruit etc. at the Homework &Aftercare program.

Ideal Charity Foundation presently run a "Feeding Scheme " for the needy children. Funding / Donations is required for a charity scheme in which the poor and disadvantaged are provided with food.
