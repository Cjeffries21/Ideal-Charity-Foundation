Our History

Ideal Charity Foundation is a non-profit organization that was founded 2020 in Riverlea,Extension,Johannesburg, South Africa. Riverlea, a historical disadvantaged, mining community identified through Ideal Charity Foundation, a Non-Profit Organization, striving towards bridging the educational gaps within our community. Ideal Charity Foundation is a Non-Profit Organization (NPO) and a Public Benefit Organisation (PBO), which manages a large number of social outreach programmes in Riverlea Extension in southern Johannesburg. The organisation aims to enable individuals and families to improve all aspects of their lives, thereby creating a lasting impact on the broader Riverlea community. Ideal Charity Foundation started operating with one project in 2020 and has grown to a network of six community-based programmes, focusing on education, vulnerable children, and work readiness. Over 2,800 people are impacted in total every year. As such, the organisation is hands-on in Riverlea, having developed close relationships with community and leaders, primary schools, which has enabled it to leverage appropriate assistance when available and necessary. The organization leads as one of the most active support groups in the Riverlea community. Community (township) development which includes programmes related to healthcare, skills development, enterprise development and education. Home based care for the Older, Frail and Disabled people. Homework & Aftercare for learners. Social visitation to community members. Toiletry drive for young girls and boys. Feeding scheme for the community. Food parcel distribution to the community. Early childhood development.

Our Vision

Sustainable Community/Township

Our vision is that through uplifting our township and eradicating poverty, people can become self-sustaining and our township can truly be economically independent. Our projects have an emphasis on young children and youth.

Our Mission

Give townships the tools to eradicate poverty

It is our mission to educate and train people within our historically disadvantaged township and ultimately give them access to the economy. People from disadvantaged townships must be able to overcome poverty no matter their race or gender. In order to do so we focus on education for young children, training for youth, and access to primary healthcare for the community.

Our Beliefs

In developing a disadvantaged mining community Riverlea, in particular a township community through a holistic community development model that includes education, skills development, enterprise development, and healthcare. The request for our efforts come from within the community, this prevents us from starting or running projects, without there being an actual need for such a project. The goal is to ensure sustainability of our project area and in particular our beneficiaries. The biggest complement we can receive is when our beneficiaries tell us that they don’t need our services anymore. In treating our beneficiaries as equals. We value their insight, concerns and comments and try to implement these within our projects to ensure excellence of our projects. This however also comes with responsibilities. Our beneficiaries are partly responsible for their own development, we provide them with a platform in which they can thrive, then the responsibility is theirs to utilize the opportunities that are laid out for them. That nothing is for “mahala” (for free). Ideal Charity Foundation is not a charity. We are not about giving a handout and don’t believe in giving people a fish but rather to teaching them how to fish in order to be independent and work for themselves. Our goal is for our township to become sustainable within 5-10 years. In serving our community with love and passion. Our love for people has moved us from working for corporate companies to working for a non-profit organisation that is determined to uplift our community. That NPOs must work more and more as social enterprises, where both the longevity of the organisation and its projects should be partially funded through income generated through the project.
